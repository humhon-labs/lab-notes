## 2. Who Is Marc Lou?

- A French-born **solo founder (solopreneur)** and indie hacker based in Bali
- Runs **15+** small startups by himself; reported monthly revenue in the **~$80K range**; crossed **$1M/year solo** in 2025; $2.2M+ in cumulative revenue

- Known for a **build-in-public** style, publishing his revenue every month

## 3. The 7 Methods (original ① + reconstructed methodology)

> ✅ **① Ship first with the minimum features (Ship Fast)** — *the item public in the original*
> Don't wait for perfection; ship an MVP within a few days to two weeks. Validate with real market response, not a spec document.

| # | Method | Key idea |
|---|--------|----------|
| **2** | Stack many, not one big hit | Rather than a single home run, keep stacking small products into a portfolio. A failure carries little risk. |
| **3** | Validate with "payments," not specs | When real payments and traffic signals appear, go all in; if there is no response, drop it fast and move on. |
| **4** | Use your personal brand as a distribution engine | Launch through your own brand (X/Twitter + newsletter), not a product account, and cross-link your products. |
| **5** | Move fast with reusable boilerplate | Don't build from scratch every time. Maximize launch speed with a starter like ShipFast. |
| **6** | Keep pricing simple — often "one-time payment" | For products where subscriptions don't fit, choose simple pricing such as one-time payments. |
| **7** | Compounding — products and audience grow each other | One product builds the audience, another builds trust; they feed each other, and momentum accumulates and transfers. |

## 4. The Core Principle Running Through It All

> 💡 The essence of Marc Lou's approach: repeat **"ship fast → validate with payments → go all in on what works, move on if it doesn't,"** and on top of that **compound a personal brand and a product portfolio.** Avoid perfectionism and over-planning; prioritize execution speed and real market signals.

- Break risk into small pieces: run several experiments in parallel instead of betting everything on one
- The validation bar is "wallets" (real payments), not "likes"
- Don't outsource marketing — accumulate your own audience as an asset
- Lower repeat costs with reusable systems
